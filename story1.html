🐊 The Big Silly Crocodile
By: Cory Williams

In a mossy corner of the Woods of Abacade, the Big Silly Crocodile woke up with a tummy-rumble that sounded like a bass drum. "I’m not just hungry," he declared, wiggling his wooden tail, "I’m Silly-Hungry!"

He marched toward the Crunchy-Leaf River, looking for a snack. Suddenly, he spotted something glowing under a bush: a Glittering Grapefruit.

"Ooh, vitamin-power!" he cheered. With one giant CHOMP, he swallowed it whole. Suddenly, his scales began to sparkle like a disco ball, and he grew two inches taller. "I feel amazing! I could eat a whole mountain!"

Next, he found a patch of Bouncing Blueberries. Each time he ate one, he didn't just grow; he started to float. Soon, the Silly Crocodile was drifting above the treetops like a green parade balloon.

"Help! I'm a cloud-croc!" he shouted to his friend Owl.

"Don't worry," Owl hooted, "you just need some Heavy Honey to bring you back down!"

The Crocodile spotted a jar of Heavy Honey sitting on a stump. He reached down, grabbed a sticky paw-full, and—THUD—he landed right back on the grass. He was now ten feet tall and very, very wide.

"I think I’m big enough for today," he giggled, patting his sparkly tummy. "Maybe tomorrow I'll just stick to one Pickle Ice Cream."

He curled up under a giant fern, dreaming of his next big, silly meal.
