🐻 Does A Bear Poo in the Woods!?
By Jonny Leighton

Deep in the emerald shadows of the forest lived Barry the Bear. Now, Barry was a very large bear with a very large problem: he was incredibly shy.

One sunny morning, after a breakfast of extra-ripe blackberries, Barry felt a familiar rumble in his tummy. It wasn't a "hungry-rumble"—it was a "gotta-go-right-now-rumble."

"Oh dear," Barry whispered, his face turning a soft shade of pink. "I need a spot. A private spot. A spot with no squirrels staring at me."

He waddled to a thicket of ferns. Perfect, he thought. But just as he started his "potty-wiggle," a Moose poked his head through the leaves. "Morning, Barry! Nice day for it!"

"Not here!" Barry squeaked, scurrying away with his legs crossed.

He tried a hollow log, but a family of Skunks was already using it for a nap. He tried a quiet cave, but it turned out to be a rehearsal space for a Woodpecker drum circle. Tap-tap-tap-tap!

"Can a bear get some peace?!" Barry cried, doing a desperate hop-skip-jump.

Finally, he spotted something strange in a clearing. It was a small wooden shack with a heart-shaped hole in the door. "An outhouse!" Barry gasped. "Luxury!"

He dashed inside, latched the door, and let out a sigh of pure relief. But just as he settled onto the wooden seat, the entire shack began to shake. VROOOOM!

A Lumberjack had hooked the outhouse to the back of his truck to move it to a new campsite! As the truck sped down the bumpy forest road, Barry bounced up and down, clutching his tummy.

The door flew open as the truck hit a pothole. Barry looked out at the passing trees, then at the terrified Lumberjack in the rearview mirror.

"Well," Barry sighed, waving a shy paw as they zoomed by, "I suppose the answer is: No, sometimes a bear poos in a moving vehicle!"
