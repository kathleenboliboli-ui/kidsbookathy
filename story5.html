🐒 The Monkeys and the Bell
by Vishnu Sharma

In a bustling city near a dense forest, a thief stole a heavy bronze bell from a temple. As he fled through the woods, he was attacked by a tiger. The thief dropped the bell and ran for his life, leaving it lying in the thick grass.

A troop of monkeys soon found the shiny object. They began to play with it, ringing it loudly day and night.

Back in the city, the people heard the constant Clang! Clang! Clang! coming from the dark woods. Because they couldn't see what was making the noise, they became terrified.

"It must be a Bell-Demon!" they whispered. "A monster that rings a bell before it eats its victims!"

Everyone was so scared they began to flee the city.

However, a wise woman named Karamala didn't believe in demons. She crept into the forest to investigate. Hiding behind a tree, she saw the truth: a group of monkeys was simply having a grand time swinging on the bell's rope!

Karamala went back to the King and said, "I can chase the Bell-Demon away, but I need a mountain of fruit and nuts to perform the ritual."

The King, desperate to save his city, gave her everything she asked for. Karamala returned to the woods and scattered the delicious food under the trees.

When the monkeys saw the treats, they dropped the bell and scrambled down to eat.
