🐊 The Enormous Crocodile
Author: Roald Dahl

The Enormous Crocodile was a very greedy crocodile who loved eating children. One day, he left the muddy river and went into the jungle to look for something tasty to eat.

“I want a juicy child for lunch!” he said with a big grin.

The crocodile thought of many clever tricks to catch children. First, he tried to pretend to be a tree so children would climb on him. But the other animals in the jungle knew his plan.

The Roly-Poly Bird warned everyone, and Trunky the Elephant spoiled his trick.

Next, the crocodile disguised himself as a playground ride. He stayed very still and waited for children to come closer. But once again, the jungle animals stopped him before he could catch anyone.

The Enormous Crocodile became angry because all his sneaky plans failed.

Finally, the animals decided they had enough of his wicked tricks. The elephant wrapped his trunk around the crocodile and threw him high into the sky.

The crocodile flew higher and higher until he landed far away—right in the hot sun, where he sizzled like a sausage!

And that was the end of the Enormous Crocodile and his nasty tricks.
